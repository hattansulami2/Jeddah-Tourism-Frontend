Historical Jeddah

Jeddah is one of the oldest and most historically significant cities in Saudi Arabia, with roots stretching back more than 2,500 years. Located on the coast of the Red Sea, it began as a small fishing village before becoming an important center for trade and commerce between Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. In 647 CE, during the rule of Caliph Othman bin Affan, Jeddah was chosen as the main port for pilgrims traveling to Mecca for Hajj and Umrah, which greatly increased its cultural and economic importance. Over time, the city developed into a diverse and multicultural hub shaped by merchants and travelers from around the world. The historic district of Al-Balad is especially famous for its unique architecture, featuring traditional houses built from coral stone collected from the Red Sea. These buildings often include large wooden balconies called “Rawasheen,” designed to improve airflow, provide shade, and maintain privacy in the hot climate. Narrow alleyways, decorative wooden windows, and intricately carved doors. Today, Jeddah remains the commercial capital of Saudi Arabia while preserving its rich heritage through the restoration and protection of Al-Balad’s historic architecture.
